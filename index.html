Definitions & Calculation Reference
Literacy % Percentage of applicable literacy sub-competencies a student (or group) has passed. A competency is passed if score ≥ 75% of its maximum marks. Literacy % = (competencies passed ÷ total applicable literacy competencies) × 100. Sub-competencies: Listening Comprehension, Oral Vocab, Picture Comprehension, Letter ID, Word Reading, Sentence Reading, Reading Comprehension, Letter / Word / Sentence Writing. ORF is excluded from this calculation.
Numeracy % Percentage of applicable numeracy sub-competencies a student (or group) has passed (score ≥ 75% of max marks per competency). Numeracy % = (competencies passed ÷ total applicable numeracy competencies) × 100. Sub-competencies: Number ID, Place Value, Pattern, Addition, Subtraction, Word Problems, Measurement, Multiplication, Shapes, Data Handling.
ORF Oral Reading Fluency — measured in correct words read per minute (cwpm). Shown as a raw score; excluded from Literacy % calculation. Grade-wise NIPUN thresholds:
· Grade 2 — NIPUN: ≥ 45 cwpm  |  Approaching: 30–44 cwpm  |  At Risk: <30 cwpm
· Grade 3 — NIPUN: ≥ 60 cwpm  |  Approaching: 45–59 cwpm  |  At Risk: <45 cwpm
Grade 1 students are not assessed on ORF.
NIPUN Student is proficient in both Literacy and Numeracy — Literacy % ≥ 75 AND Numeracy % ≥ 75. The student has passed ≥ 75% of competencies in each subject independently.
Approaching Student is proficient in at least one subject (Literacy or Numeracy) but not both — Literacy % or Numeracy % is between 50%–74% in at least one area. Needs targeted support to cross the NIPUN threshold.
At Risk Student has at least one subject below the proficiency threshold — scored below 50% on at least one competency area (Literacy or Numeracy). Requires urgent, intensive intervention.
High Priority School-level intervention priority. Fewer than 40% of assessed students in the school are NIPUN. Requires immediate and intensive instructional support.
Medium Priority School-level intervention priority. Between 40%–74% of assessed students in the school are NIPUN. Needs close monitoring and targeted practice to push students across the threshold.
Low Priority School-level intervention priority. 75% or more of assessed students in the school are NIPUN. School is on track — continue maintaining quality and address remaining gaps.
